Question 24.
Please give an example of covariant shift? [1]

<details><summary><b>Answer</b></summary>
1.  Let's consider a scenario where you're building a model to classify images of cats and dogs. During training, the images come from a specific source with certain characteristics like background, lighting, and resolution. Now, if the model is tested on images from a different source with distinct characteristics, such as different backgrounds or lighting conditions, that is an example of covariate shift. The shift in image characteristics between training and testing can impact the model's performance negatively due to the distribution mismatch. Techniques like domain adaptation or batch normalisation are employed to mitigate covariate shift effects.

</details>

Regularisation techniques and validation sets are commonly used to address overfitting.
